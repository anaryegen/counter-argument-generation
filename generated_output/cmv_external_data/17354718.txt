How Language Use Can Empower Women: The Discourse on Gender-Equitable Language in Germany

Gundula Gwenn Hiller9

First Online: 09 July 2023

This chapter shows that language is a powerful tool for gender equality and thus for the sustainable empowerment of women. It argues that social realities are reflected in language and that changes in the societal mindset are interconnected with language use. Research findings support the claim proposed by feminist linguistics that gender-equitable language use makes women more visible and can open up new career opportunities for them by encouraging increased mental representation of prestigious professions. Using the example of the public discourse on gender language in Germany, this chapter will show on the one hand how language reflects and perpetuates sexist power structures, and on the other hand how these structures can be softened by persistent counteraction by female academics and activists, resulting in a more gender-equitable language.
